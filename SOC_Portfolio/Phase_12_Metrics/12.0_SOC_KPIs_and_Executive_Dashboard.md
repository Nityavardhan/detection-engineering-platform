# SOC Metrics & Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** June 2026
**Role:** SOC Manager / CISO Office

---

## 1. Overview

Metrics are how a SOC proves its value to the business. Without measurable KPIs, the SOC is a cost center with no way to justify its budget. This document defines the core operational and strategic metrics that our SOC tracks.

---

## 2. Operational KPIs

These metrics measure the SOC's day-to-day effectiveness and are reviewed **weekly** by the SOC Manager.

### 2.1 Mean Time to Detect (MTTD)
- **Definition:** The average elapsed time between when a threat first appears in the environment and when the SOC detects it.
- **Target:** < 15 minutes for Critical, < 1 hour for High.
- **Formula:** `MTTD = Σ(Detection Timestamp - Attack Timestamp) / Total Incidents`
- **Why it matters:** A lower MTTD means the adversary has less time to operate undetected. Industry average is ~200 days for APTs; our goal is minutes.

### 2.2 Mean Time to Respond (MTTR)
- **Definition:** The average elapsed time between when the SOC detects a threat and when containment is initiated.
- **Target:** < 30 minutes for Critical, < 4 hours for High.
- **Formula:** `MTTR = Σ(Containment Timestamp - Detection Timestamp) / Total Incidents`
- **Why it matters:** Detection without response is useless. MTTR measures how quickly the team can stop the bleeding.

### 2.3 Alert Volume
- **Definition:** Total number of alerts generated by the SIEM per day/week.
- **Target:** Monitor for trends. A sudden 300% spike may indicate an active attack or a misconfigured rule.
- **Breakdown by severity:**
  - Critical: Should be < 5/week (if higher, rules need tuning or the environment is under attack).
  - High: 10–50/week.
  - Medium: 50–200/week.
  - Low/Informational: 200+/week (used for Threat Hunting baselines).

### 2.4 False Positive Rate
- **Definition:** Percentage of alerts that, upon investigation, turned out to be benign.
- **Target:** < 30%.
- **Formula:** `FP Rate = (False Positive Alerts / Total Alerts) × 100`
- **Why it matters:** A high false positive rate causes alert fatigue, which causes analysts to start ignoring alerts, which causes them to miss the one real attack buried in the noise.

### 2.5 Alert Closure Rate
- **Definition:** Percentage of alerts that are triaged and closed within their SLA window.
- **Target:** > 95%.
- **Formula:** `Closure Rate = (Alerts Closed Within SLA / Total Alerts) × 100`

## 3. Strategic KPIs

These metrics measure the SOC's long-term maturity and are reviewed **monthly** by the CISO.

### 3.1 MITRE ATT&CK Coverage
- **Definition:** Percentage of MITRE ATT&CK techniques for which the SOC has at least one active detection rule.
- **Target:** > 60% coverage of relevant techniques.
- **Current:** Our 20 detection rules cover techniques across 8 out of 14 ATT&CK tactics.
- **Visualization:** MITRE ATT&CK Navigator heatmap (already generated by our pipeline).

### 3.2 Compliance Coverage
- **Definition:** Percentage of required framework controls (NIST CSF, ISO 27001, CIS v8) that are actively monitored or enforced.
- **Current:**
  - NIST CSF: 6/6 functions covered (100%).
  - CIS Controls v8: 12/18 control groups covered (67%).
  - ISO 27001 Annex A: 16 controls addressed.

### 3.3 Risk Score Trend
- **Definition:** The average residual risk score across the Risk Register, tracked over time.
- **Target:** Decreasing quarter-over-quarter.
- **Current Average:** 5.0 (Medium) — derived from our 10-item Risk Register.

### 3.4 Incidents by Category
- **Definition:** Breakdown of confirmed incidents by type (e.g., Malware, Phishing, Brute Force).
- **Why it matters:** If 60% of incidents are phishing, the CISO can justify investing in email security and security awareness training.

## 4. Executive Dashboard Specification

This dashboard is designed for the CISO and Board. It should be updated weekly and present the following at a glance:

| Widget | Type | Data Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| MTTD (Current Month) | Single Metric (gauge) | Incident Tracker |
| MTTR (Current Month) | Single Metric (gauge) | Incident Tracker |
| Total Alerts (7-Day) | Metric with trend arrow | SIEM (Elasticsearch) |
| Alerts by Severity | Pie/Donut Chart | SIEM |
| Top 5 MITRE Tactics Detected | Horizontal Bar | SIEM + ATT&CK mapping |
| NIST CSF Coverage | Radar Chart | Control Matrix |
| Open High/Critical Risks | Data Table | Risk Register |
| False Positive Rate (Trend) | Line Chart (12-week) | SOC Ticket System |
| Detection Rule Efficacy (Top 10) | Bar Chart | SIEM alert data |
| Compliance Score | Gauge (0–100%) | Control Matrix |

## 5. Reporting Cadence

| Report | Audience | Frequency | Content |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Daily Ops Brief | SOC Team | Daily | Alert count, open tickets, shift handoff notes |
| Weekly SOC Report | SOC Manager | Weekly | KPIs, alert trends, notable incidents |
| Monthly Executive Summary | CISO / Board | Monthly | Strategic KPIs, risk posture, compliance score, MITRE coverage |
| Quarterly Risk Review | CISO / Board / Legal | Quarterly | Full Risk Register review, BIA update, policy compliance |
