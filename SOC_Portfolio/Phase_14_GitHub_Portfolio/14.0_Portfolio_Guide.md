## 3. Interview Questions & Answers (All Phases)

### Architecture & Planning
**Q: "Walk me through how you designed your SOC."**
> Start with the Project Charter (business objectives), then Architecture (Elastic Stack on Ubuntu, Sysmon on Windows, Filebeat on Linux), then the Threat Model (identifying the adversaries), and finally the detection rules driven by that model.

### Detection Engineering
**Q: "How many detection rules do you have and how do you organize them?"**
> 20 rules organized by MITRE ATT&CK tactic: Credential Access, Execution, Persistence, Defense Evasion, and Lateral Movement. Each rule includes KQL, false positive analysis, and an investigation guide.

### Incident Response
**Q: "If ransomware hit your network, what is the first thing you do?"**
> Disconnect affected hosts. Do NOT power off. Disable shared drives to prevent lateral encryption. Alert executive team and legal. Follow IR Playbook 4.

### Forensics
**Q: "You arrive at a compromised machine. Walk me through your process."**
> Order of Volatility: capture RAM first, then disk image. Hash the image (SHA-256). Start Chain of Custody log. Analyze using Chainsaw for rapid Sigma rule triage. Build forensic timeline.

### GRC
**Q: "How do you justify the SOC's budget to the board?"**
> I present the Executive Dashboard showing MTTD, MTTR, MITRE ATT&CK coverage percentage, compliance score, and the Risk Register showing how our controls have reduced residual risk from High to Medium.

### Compliance
**Q: "How do you prove compliance during an audit?"**
> I present the Security Control Matrix. Every detection rule and policy is mapped to the specific NIST CSF function, ISO 27001 Annex A control, and CIS safeguard it satisfies. The auditor gets a single document with evidence references.
