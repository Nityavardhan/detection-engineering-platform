# Enterprise Security Policies

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** June 2026
**Role:** GRC Analyst / CISO Office
**Classification:** Internal / Confidential

---

## 1. Overview
Security Policies are formal, management-approved documents that dictate how the organization protects its information assets. They translate business objectives into enforceable security requirements. Every enterprise SOC must operate within a defined policy framework.

This document contains seven core security policies required for our simulated enterprise.

---

## Policy 1: Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

**Purpose:** Defines the acceptable and prohibited uses of organizational IT resources.

**Scope:** All employees, contractors, and third-party users with access to company systems.

**Key Requirements:**
- Company-issued devices shall be used for authorized business purposes only.
- Users shall not install unauthorized software without IT approval.
- Users shall not attempt to circumvent security controls (e.g., disabling antivirus, using personal VPN).
- Sharing of login credentials is strictly prohibited.
- Violation of this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

---

## Policy 2: Password Policy

**Purpose:** Establishes minimum standards for password complexity and management.

**Key Requirements:**
- Minimum password length: **14 characters**.
- Must include at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character.
- Passwords must be changed every **90 days**.
- Password history: The last **12 passwords** cannot be reused.
- Account lockout: After **5 consecutive failed attempts**, the account is locked for **30 minutes**.
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is **mandatory** for all administrative and remote access accounts.
- Passwords must not be stored in plaintext. All systems must use salted hashing (e.g., bcrypt, Argon2).

---

## Policy 3: Access Control Policy

**Purpose:** Ensures users are granted the minimum level of access required to perform their job duties (Principle of Least Privilege).

**Key Requirements:**
- All access requests must be approved by the user's manager and the asset owner.
- Access reviews shall be conducted **quarterly** to remove stale accounts and unnecessary permissions.
- Privileged accounts (Domain Admins, root) shall use dedicated credentials separate from daily-use accounts.
- Service accounts must not use interactive logon and must be documented in the Asset Inventory.
- Remote access (VPN, RDP) requires MFA.
- Terminated employees must have all access revoked within **4 hours** of their departure notification.

---

## Policy 4: Incident Response Policy

**Purpose:** Mandates the establishment and maintenance of an Incident Response capability.

**Key Requirements:**
- The organization shall maintain a documented Incident Response Plan aligned with NIST SP 800-61.
- An Incident Response Team (IRT) shall be designated with clearly defined roles and escalation paths.
- All security incidents must be reported to the SOC within **1 hour** of discovery.
- Severity classification:
  - **Critical:** Active data breach, ransomware, credential dumping. Response within **15 minutes**.
  - **High:** Brute force, privilege escalation, malware. Response within **1 hour**.
  - **Medium:** Phishing attempts, suspicious activity. Response within **4 hours**.
  - **Low:** Policy violations, informational alerts. Response within **24 hours**.
- Post-incident reviews (Lessons Learned) are mandatory for all Critical and High severity incidents.

---

## Policy 5: Logging and Monitoring Policy

**Purpose:** Ensures that all critical systems generate and retain security-relevant logs.

**Key Requirements:**
- All systems in scope (servers, endpoints, network devices) must forward logs to the centralized SIEM.
- The following log sources are **mandatory**: Authentication events, Process execution, Network connections, Administrative actions.
- Logs must be retained for a minimum of **90 days** online (hot storage) and **1 year** in archive (cold storage).
- Log integrity must be protected. Unauthorized clearing of security logs is a **Critical** security incident (see IR Playbook 6).
- SIEM alert rules must be reviewed and updated **quarterly**.

---

## Policy 6: Vulnerability Management Policy

**Purpose:** Ensures timely identification and remediation of security vulnerabilities across all systems.

**Key Requirements:**
- Vulnerability scans shall be conducted **monthly** on all internal and external-facing systems.
- Remediation SLAs:
  - **Critical (CVSS 9.0–10.0):** Patch within **72 hours**.
  - **High (CVSS 7.0–8.9):** Patch within **14 days**.
  - **Medium (CVSS 4.0–6.9):** Patch within **30 days**.
  - **Low (CVSS 0.1–3.9):** Patch within **90 days**.
- Exceptions must be formally documented with a compensating control and approved by the CISO.
- All patches must be tested in a staging environment before production deployment.

---

## Policy 7: Backup and Recovery Policy

**Purpose:** Ensures critical business data can be restored in the event of a disaster or security incident.

**Key Requirements:**
- All critical systems (as defined in the BIA) must have automated backups configured.
- Backup frequency must meet or exceed the RPO defined in the Business Impact Analysis.
- Backups must be encrypted (AES-256) both in transit and at rest.
- At least one backup copy must be stored **off-site** or in the cloud.
- **Air-gapped backups** are mandatory for protection against ransomware.
- Backup restore tests must be conducted **quarterly** and documented with success/failure outcomes.
- Backup logs must be monitored for failures. Consecutive backup failures are a **High** severity alert.
